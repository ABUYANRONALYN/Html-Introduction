My Skills

  • INTRAPERSONAL
    Interpersonal pertains to interactions and relationships between people. It involves skills and behaviors that facilitate effective communication and collaboration.
    For example, strong interpersonal skills include the ability to empathize, negotiate, and resolve conflicts with others.
  • ADAPTABILITY
    Adaptability refers to the ability to adjust to new conditions or changes in the environment effectively. It involves being flexible and open to new ideas or situations,
    allowing a person or organization to respond to challenges and shifts with ease.
  • DETAIL-ORIENTED
    Detail-oriented describes a person who pays close attention to the small aspects of tasks or projects. They are meticulous in ensuring that every component is accurate
    and complete, often catching errors or inconsistencies that others might overlook. This trait is valuable in roles that require precision and thoroughness.
  • SELF-MOTIVATION
    Self-motivation is the drive to take initiative and pursue goals independently without external encouragement or rewards. It involves having internal reasons, such as
    personal satisfaction or a sense of purpose, that propel you to stay focused and work towards objectives.
